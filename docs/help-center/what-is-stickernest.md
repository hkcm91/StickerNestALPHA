# What Is StickerNest?

StickerNest is a spatial platform where you build interactive spaces on an infinite canvas. Think of it as a desktop where stickers are your icons, widgets are your apps, and everything lives on a shared, zoomable surface that you can collaborate on in real time.

## The Canvas

Your canvas is an infinite 2D workspace. You can pan around, zoom in and out, and place anything anywhere. There are no fixed grids or page boundaries — just open space for you to organize however you like.

Each canvas is its own space. You might use one canvas as a project dashboard, another as a mood board, another as a game table. Canvases can be private, shared with collaborators, or published publicly with a custom URL.

## Stickers

Stickers are the visual building blocks of your canvas. They're images, GIFs, or videos that you place on the surface. Stickers don't run code or do anything interactive — they're purely visual. Use them to decorate, organize, label, or just make your space feel like yours.

You can upload your own stickers or browse the built-in gallery.

## Widgets

Widgets are the interactive part. Each widget is a small app that lives on your canvas: a sticky note, a timer, a weather display, a data table, a game — anything a creator can dream up.

Widgets come from the Marketplace, where creators publish them for anyone to install. You can also build your own using the Widget Lab (available on Creator tier and above).

Widgets can talk to each other through pipelines — visual connections you draw between them. One widget's output becomes another widget's input. This is how you build complex interactive flows without writing code.

## Collaboration

StickerNest is multiplayer by default. When you share a canvas, collaborators see each other's cursors in real time, can move things around together, and co-edit documents simultaneously. Changes sync instantly — no refresh needed.

You control who can do what with four permission levels: Owner (full control), Editor (can modify everything), Commenter (can annotate but not change content), and Viewer (read-only).

## Modes

Every canvas has two modes:

**Edit mode** is where you build and arrange your space. You can move entities, resize widgets, draw pipelines, and configure everything. This is the workspace view.

**Preview mode** is the finished product. The layout locks in place and widgets become fully interactive. This is what viewers and embedded canvases see — a polished, interactive space.

You can toggle between modes with the mode switch in the toolbar, or press `P` as a shortcut.

## Spatial / VR

If you have a VR headset (like Quest 3), you can step into your canvas in three dimensions. Your 2D canvas becomes a 3D environment where widgets float in space and you interact with them using VR controllers. This is experimental but fully supported.
