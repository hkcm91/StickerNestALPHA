# Stickers and Assets

Stickers are the visual layer of your canvas — images, GIFs, and videos that you place to decorate, organize, and personalize your space. Unlike widgets, stickers don't run code or respond to events. They're purely visual.

## Adding Stickers

Open the **Asset Panel** (left sidebar, image icon, or press `A`) and switch to the Stickers tab. You have two options:

**Browse the gallery** — scroll or search through the built-in sticker library. Drag any sticker onto your canvas to place it.

**Upload your own** — click the upload button or drag a file directly into the asset panel. Supported formats: PNG, JPG, GIF, SVG, WebP, and MP4/WebM for video stickers.

## Manipulating Stickers

Stickers behave like any canvas entity:

- **Move**: click and drag
- **Resize**: drag corner or edge handles (hold Shift for aspect ratio lock)
- **Rotate**: drag the rotation handle above the selection
- **Z-order**: right-click for stacking options (Bring to Front, Send to Back, etc.)
- **Delete**: select and press Delete or Backspace

## Stickers vs Widgets

This is an important distinction:

**Stickers** are static visual assets. They look like images on your canvas. They have no interactivity, no configuration panel, and no saved state. They're fast and lightweight.

**Widgets** are interactive programs. They have configuration, state persistence, event ports, and can communicate with other widgets. They're more powerful but heavier.

If you just need a picture, label, or decoration — use a sticker. If you need interactivity — use a widget.

## Managing Your Gallery

The Gallery panel shows all assets in your workspace — both uploaded stickers and any media files associated with your canvases. You can:

- **Search** by name or tags
- **Tag** assets for organization
- **Delete** assets you no longer need
- **View details** including dimensions, file size, and upload date

Uploaded assets are stored per-workspace and available across all canvases in that workspace.
