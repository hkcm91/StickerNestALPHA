# Creating Your First Canvas

This walkthrough takes you from signing up to having a working canvas with widgets and stickers — about five minutes.

## 1. Sign Up

Create an account at stickernest.com using your email or sign in with Google, GitHub, or Discord. You'll land on your workspace home page.

## 2. Create a Canvas

Click **New Canvas** from the workspace home. Give it a name — you can always rename it later. Your new empty canvas opens in edit mode.

## 3. Look Around

Your canvas is infinite. Here's how to navigate:

- **Pan**: Click and drag on empty space, or hold Space and drag
- **Zoom**: Scroll wheel, or pinch on trackpad
- **Zoom to fit**: Press `Shift+1` to zoom out to see everything

The toolbar at the top shows your current tool, zoom level, and the edit/preview mode toggle.

## 4. Add a Sticker

Open the asset panel (click the image icon in the left sidebar, or press `A`). Browse the sticker gallery or upload your own image. Drag a sticker onto the canvas — it drops wherever you release.

You can resize stickers by selecting them and dragging the corner handles. Hold Shift while dragging to keep the proportions locked.

## 5. Install a Widget

Open the Marketplace from the left sidebar. Browse or search for a widget — try "Sticky Note" or "Timer" to start. Click **Install**, then drag the widget from your asset panel onto the canvas.

Widgets are interactive. Click on a placed widget to interact with it (in edit mode, double-click to enter the widget; in preview mode, single-click works).

## 6. Configure a Widget

Select a widget and open the properties panel (right sidebar). You'll see configuration options that the widget creator defined — things like colors, labels, data sources, or behavior settings. Changes apply immediately.

## 7. Connect Widgets with a Pipeline

If you want two widgets to talk to each other:

1. Switch to the pipeline tool (press `W` or select it from the toolbar)
2. Click an output port on one widget (shown as a small circle on the right side)
3. Drag to an input port on another widget (small circle on the left side)
4. Release to create the connection

Now when the first widget emits an event, the second widget receives it. For example, a button widget could trigger a counter widget to increment.

## 8. Switch to Preview Mode

Click the mode toggle in the toolbar (or press `P`) to switch to preview mode. Your layout locks, and widgets become fully interactive. This is what your audience will see.

Switch back to edit mode the same way when you want to make changes.

## 9. Share Your Canvas

Click the **Share** button in the top bar. You can:

- **Invite by email**: Send an invite link with a specific role (Editor, Commenter, or Viewer)
- **Copy link**: Get a shareable URL
- **Publish**: Make the canvas publicly accessible at a custom slug URL

## Tips

- **Undo/Redo**: `Cmd/Ctrl+Z` and `Cmd/Ctrl+Shift+Z` work throughout
- **Multi-select**: Hold Shift and click multiple items, or drag a selection box around them
- **Delete**: Select items and press Delete or Backspace
- **Duplicate**: `Cmd/Ctrl+D` duplicates selected items
- **Save**: Canvases auto-save. You'll see a save indicator in the top bar.
