## Twitter/X Thread

**Tweet 1 (Hook — attach a screenshot or canvas recording):**

I'm building a spatial operating system.

The canvas is the desktop. Stickers are icons. Widgets are apps. Pipelines are IPC.

It's called StickerNest — and it runs in your browser, in 3D, and in VR.

Here's what that actually means 🧵

**Tweet 2 (The Canvas):**

Start with an infinite canvas. Not a whiteboard — a workspace.

You can place stickers, drop in interactive widgets, write notes, embed media. Everything lives in one spatial environment that you control.

It's your digital room.

**Tweet 3 (Widgets = Apps):**

Widgets are the core.

They're sandboxed programs — built with plain HTML/CSS/JS — that run inside the canvas. A clock, a to-do list, a data dashboard, a game. Anything.

They talk to each other through a typed event bus. One widget's output becomes another's input.

**Tweet 4 (Pipelines):**

That's where pipelines come in.

You visually wire widget outputs to widget inputs — like a node graph. No code required.

A form widget can feed into a filter, which feeds into a chart. Drag, connect, done.

**Tweet 5 (Surfaces):**

Same canvas. Three surfaces.

- 2D infinite canvas in your browser
- 3D spatial view
- Full VR on Quest 3

Build once, run everywhere. Same widgets, same data, same pipelines.

**Tweet 6 (Build-in-public + personal):**

I'm a solo self-taught dev building this from scratch.

V4 taught me what works and what breaks. V5 is a clean-sheet rewrite with a 7-layer architecture designed to keep things from falling apart as it grows.

I'll be sharing the journey here — the wins, the bugs, all of it.

**Tweet 7 (CTA):**

If any of this sounds interesting — follow along.

I'm building StickerNest in the open and I'd love your feedback as it takes shape.

More soon. ✌️

#buildinpublic #webdev
