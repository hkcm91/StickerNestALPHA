# Sample Builder Profiles

Three example profiles showing the range of builders Mirror captures.
These demonstrate the format and depth expected from a full interview.

## Profile 1: Technical Builder, No Money

# Builder Profile — Alex

*Last updated: 2026-03-15*

## Skills & Tools
- JavaScript/TypeScript — expert
- React — expert
- Node.js — comfortable
- Python — comfortable
- Figma — comfortable
- Claude Code — comfortable
- AWS (basic Lambda, S3) — learning
- SQL/PostgreSQL — comfortable
- Git/GitHub — expert

## Constraints
- **Time available:** 15 hours/week (evenings after day job, full Saturdays)
- **Budget:** $200 total (already paying for domain + hosting)
- **Location:** Remote, US Eastern timezone
- **Equipment:** MacBook Pro, second monitor, decent mic
- **Schedule:** Weekday evenings 7-10pm, Saturdays 9am-5pm

## Passions
Makes time disappear: building tools that solve annoying workflows. Loves the
moment when a clunky manual process becomes one click. Could talk for hours
about developer experience and automation.

## Fight
Frustrated that small businesses still use spreadsheets for everything because
"real" software costs $200/month and requires a 30-minute onboarding call.
Believes useful tools should be simple and affordable.

## Unique Understanding
Spent 3 years at a small agency building internal tools for clients. Understands
the gap between what non-technical business owners describe wanting and what they
actually need. Has seen dozens of small business workflows up close.

## Anti-Skills
- Sales calls — hates phone calls of any kind, will not do outbound sales
- Content creation — can write docs but not blog posts, tweets, or marketing copy
- Visual design — can implement designs but can't create them from scratch
- Project management — loses interest in coordinating other people

## Past Projects & Patterns
- **Chrome extension for tab management** — built it, shipped it, got 200 users,
  stopped maintaining it because didn't know how to grow it
- **Freelance web dev** — did 3 projects, hated the client communication part,
  stopped taking clients
- **Open source contribution** — contributed to a popular React library, enjoyed
  the technical work but lost motivation without revenue

**Pattern:** Builds well, ships, but stalls at marketing/growth. Technical
execution is strong; distribution is the consistent weakness.

## Financial Reality
- **Monthly gap:** Needs $1,500/month additional income to stop living paycheck-to-paycheck
- **Timeline:** No hard deadline, but would love something generating income in 6 months
- **"Enough" number:** $3,000/month would be life-changing
- **Current income sources:** Full-time frontend developer job ($65K/year)

## Hobbies & Interests
- Mechanical keyboards (active in r/MechanicalKeyboards)
- Home automation (runs Home Assistant, tinkers constantly)
- Board games (weekly game night, knows the hobby board game community well)

## Deal-Breakers
- No phone calls or video sales calls
- No managing freelancers or employees
- No ongoing client relationships (prefer build-once, sell-many)
- No social media content creation as a growth strategy

## Profile 2: Creative Person, No Tech Skills

# Builder Profile — Maria

*Last updated: 2026-03-15*

## Skills & Tools
- Illustration (digital) — expert (Procreate, Clip Studio Paint)
- Graphic design — comfortable (Canva Pro, basic InDesign)
- Photography — comfortable (iPhone, Lightroom mobile)
- Notion — comfortable (uses for personal organization)
- Social media — comfortable (Instagram, Pinterest)
- Video editing — learning (CapCut)
- HTML/CSS — aware (can modify templates but not build from scratch)

## Constraints
- **Time available:** 25 hours/week (not currently employed full-time)
- **Budget:** $100/month ongoing, $500 upfront
- **Location:** Portland, OR — willing to do local events
- **Equipment:** iPad Pro with Apple Pencil, iPhone 15, laptop (older MacBook Air)
- **Schedule:** Flexible weekdays, needs weekends for family

## Passions
Drawing characters and patterns. Can spend entire days in Procreate and not
notice. Loves creating visual worlds — has sketchbooks full of original
characters and environments. Obsessed with stationery and paper goods.

## Fight
Angry that independent artists get exploited by print-on-demand platforms that
take 70% margins. Believes artists should be able to sell directly without
needing to become full-stack developers or marketing experts.

## Unique Understanding
As a former elementary art teacher (5 years), understands how to break complex
creative processes into simple steps anyone can follow. Also deeply understands
what parents want (wholesome, educational, screen-free activities for kids).

## Anti-Skills
- Coding of any kind — tried learning JavaScript twice, hated it both times
- Spreadsheets and data analysis — eyes glaze over
- Long-form writing — can write captions and short descriptions but not articles
- Negotiation — underprices everything, uncomfortable discussing money

## Past Projects & Patterns
- **Etsy shop (stickers)** — sold ~$400/month for 8 months, burned out on
  fulfillment (printing, cutting, shipping every day)
- **Instagram art account** — grew to 3K followers, couldn't figure out how to
  turn followers into revenue
- **Children's book project** — wrote and illustrated 80% of it, got overwhelmed
  by the publishing process, shelved it

**Pattern:** Creates beautiful work, builds small audiences, but gets stuck on
the business/logistics side. Fulfillment and business operations are the wall.

## Financial Reality
- **Monthly gap:** Needs $2,000/month (partner's income covers basics but it's tight)
- **Timeline:** 3-4 months before needing to find a "regular" job
- **"Enough" number:** $2,500/month — would let her stay independent
- **Current income sources:** Sporadic freelance illustration ($200-400/month),
  partner's income covers rent

## Hobbies & Interests
- Journaling and planner communities (very active in r/bulletjournal and planner
  Facebook groups)
- Cottagecore aesthetic community
- Local craft fairs (attends as customer, has considered selling)
- Collecting vintage children's book illustrations

## Deal-Breakers
- No coding or building software
- No physical product fulfillment (learned from Etsy burnout)
- No corporate clients or "professional" design work
- No cold outreach or networking events

## Profile 3: Money But No Time

# Builder Profile — James

*Last updated: 2026-03-15*

## Skills & Tools
- Product management — expert (10 years in tech)
- Data analysis — comfortable (SQL, basic Python, Looker)
- No-code tools — comfortable (Bubble, Airtable, Zapier, Make)
- Copywriting — comfortable (years of writing PRDs and marketing briefs)
- User research — comfortable (conducting interviews, surveys)
- Figma — learning (can navigate but not design)
- AI tools — comfortable (Claude, ChatGPT, Midjourney for mockups)

## Constraints
- **Time available:** 5-7 hours/week (demanding full-time job, two young kids)
- **Budget:** $5,000 upfront, $500/month ongoing
- **Location:** San Francisco Bay Area — high cost of living
- **Equipment:** MacBook Pro (work), personal laptop, iPhone
- **Schedule:** Unpredictable — some late nights, occasional weekend morning blocks

## Passions
Obsessed with systems and efficiency. Loves finding the 80/20 — the minimum
input that produces maximum output. Gets excited about finding underserved
markets where a simple solution could win. Reads business strategy books
constantly.

## Fight
Frustrated that B2B SaaS has become so bloated and expensive. Small businesses
pay for 100 features to use 3. Believes there's a massive opportunity in
unbundled, single-purpose tools that do one thing well.

## Unique Understanding
Ten years of product management at three different B2B SaaS companies. Has seen
from the inside how enterprise software gets bloated (feature creep driven by
sales team requests from big accounts). Understands exactly which features
small businesses actually use vs. what they're forced to pay for. Deep network
in the SF tech scene.

## Anti-Skills
- Consistent daily execution — can do intense sprints but not steady daily work
- Visual design — zero aesthetic sense
- Customer support — impatient with repetitive questions
- Social media — finds it mind-numbing, will not post regularly

## Past Projects & Patterns
- **Micro-SaaS idea #1** — validated demand for a Slack standup bot, got halfway
  through building it in Bubble, realized 5 competitors launched while he was
  building. Abandoned.
- **Info product** — outlined a course on "PM skills for founders," wrote 3 of 8
  modules, never finished
- **Newsletter** — wrote 12 weekly issues about B2B SaaS trends, had 400
  subscribers, stopped because it felt like a second job with no revenue

**Pattern:** Strong at identifying opportunities and starting, but time
constraints mean slow execution. By the time he ships, the window has often
closed. Needs businesses that can launch fast or don't have tight timing windows.

## Financial Reality
- **Monthly gap:** Not urgent — has a good salary ($190K). Looking for $2,000-5,000/month
  passive income to build toward eventual independence
- **Timeline:** No pressure — 12-18 month runway to build something
- **"Enough" number:** $10,000/month would let him leave his job
- **Current income sources:** Full-time PM salary, ~$50K in savings earmarked
  for business investment

## Hobbies & Interests
- Angel investing (small checks in 4 startups, follows the space closely)
- Youth soccer coaching (daughters' teams)
- Woodworking (beginner, finds it meditative)
- r/SaaS and Indie Hackers community (reads daily but rarely posts)

## Deal-Breakers
- No businesses requiring daily content creation
- No client services or consulting (time constraints make this impossible)
- No businesses requiring team management in the first year
- No physical products
